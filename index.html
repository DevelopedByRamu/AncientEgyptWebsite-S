Pharaoh of Egypt

Meet

Ramsees II

Born into the royal family around 1303 BCE, Ramesses II, later known as Ramesses the Great, rose to become one of ancient Egypt's most celebrated and longest-reigning pharaohs. Appointed as prince regent and commander of the army by his father Seti I, he demonstrated military brilliance from a young age before ascending to the throne.

His 66-year reign was marked by unprecedented construction projects, military campaigns that stretched Egypt's boundaries, and diplomatic achievements that forged lasting peace with rival powers. His legacy endures in the monuments he built and the stories carved in stone across the Nile Valley.
